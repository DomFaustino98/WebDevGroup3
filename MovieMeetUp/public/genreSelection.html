Main Movie Genres

Action

Definition

One of the earliest film genres in existence, the action genre has close ties to classic strife and struggle narratives that you find across all manner of art and literature. Includes everything from historical war epics to some basic portrayals of dastardly train robberies. It’s also one of our best Examples of the evolution of our cinematic hero’s journey and the classic hero vs. villain narratives, which you’ll find across cinema and genres.

Examples:
Bourne Ultimatum
The Matrix
Die Hard

Comedy

Definition

Everyone needs a good laugh, and the comedy genre is one of the best ways to get there. The comedy genre covers any work in film or television whose general purpose is to create humor and intentional laughs for the audience. The comedy genre has also been one of the most flexible genres, as its roots have made their way into the very fabric of cinema.



Examples:
Borat
Tropic Thunder
Superbad

Drama

Definition

The drama genre features stories with high stakes and numerous conflicts. They’re plot-driven and demand that every character and scene move the story forward. Dramas follow a clearly defined narrative plot structure, portraying real-life scenarios or extreme situations with emotionally-driven characters.




Examples:
Schindler's List
The Social Network
Titanic

Fantasy

Definition

Films in the fantasy genre feature magical and supernatural elements that do not exist in the real world. Although some films juxtapose a real-world setting with fantastical elements, many create entirely imaginary universes with their own laws, logic, and populations of imaginary races and creatures. Like science fiction films, fantasy films are speculative but are not tied to reality or scientific fact.


Examples:
Labyrinth
The Wizard of Oz
Harry Potter

Horror

Definition

Horror films feature elements that leave people with an overwhelming sense of fear and dread. Horror movies often include serial killers or monsters as persistent, evil antagonists to play on viewers’ fears or nightmares. Audiences who love the horror genre seek out these movies specifically for the adrenaline rush produced by ghosts, gore, monsters, and jump-scares.



Examples:
Friday the 13th
A Nightmare on Elm Street
Halloween

Mystery

Definition

Mystery films are all about the puzzle, and often feature a detective or amateur sleuth who is trying to solve it. Mystery films are full of suspense, and the protagonist searches for clues or evidence throughout the movie, piecing together events and interviewing suspects to solve the central question.




Examples:
Knives Out
Sherlock Holmes
Zodiac

Romance

Definition

Romance films are love stories. They center around two protagonists exploring some of the elements of love like relationships, sacrifice, marriage, obsession, or destruction. Romance movies sometimes feature hardships like illness, infidelity, tragedy, or other obstacles for the love interests to overcome.




Examples:
The Fault in Our Stars
The Notebook
The Lucky One

Thriller

Definition

Thrillers expertly blend mystery, tension, and anticipation into one exciting story. Successful thrillers are well-paced, often introducing red herrings, divulging plot twists, and revealing information at the exact right moments to keep the audience intrigued. Thrillers often include a “ticking clock” aspect, where the stakes are set against a finite amount of time. Crime films, political thrillers, and techno-thrillers are all featured in the thriller genre.

Examples:
The Silence of the Lambs
Parasite
Fight Club

Western

Definition

Westerns tell the tale of a cowboy or gunslinger pursuing an outlaw in the Wild West. The main character is often seeking revenge and will face off with the criminal in a duel or shootout at the end. Westerns are vivid productions set in the American West—such as the desert, mountains, or plains—that can inspire and inform the characters and the action.



Examples:
Once Upon a Time in the West
The Good, The Bad, and the Ugly
True Grit

Science Fiction

Definition

Science fiction (sometimes shortened to sci-fi or SF) is a genre of speculative fiction that typically deals with imaginative and futuristic concepts such as advanced science and technology, space exploration, time travel, parallel universes, and extraterrestrial life.





Examples:
War of the Worlds
Frankenstein
The Invisible Man

Main Sub Genres

Action Genre

Military/War

Definition

Concerned with warfare, typically about naval, air, or land battles, with combat scenes central to the drama. The fateful nature of battle scenes means that war films often end with them.


Examples:
Hacksaw Ridge
1917
Dunkirk

Spy & Espionage

Definition

Spy films show the espionage activities of government agents and their risk of being discovered by their enemies.





Examples:
Goldfinger
Dr.No
The Man from U.N.C.L.E.

Martial Arts

Definition

Martial arts films, also colloquially known as karate or kung fu films, are a subgenre of action films that feature numerous martial arts fights between characters



Examples:
Five Deadly Venoms
Enter the Dragon
Ip Man

Western Gun

Definition

Emphasizes gunplay, action, and often violence.







Examples:
Colt .45
Five Guns West
the Gunfighter

Action Hybrid

Definition

This genre is for movies that highly emphasize action as much as it emphasizes its hybrid genre.




Examples:
Space Jam
Hellboy
Creed

Comedy

Slapstick

Definition

Slapstick comedy is primarily a physical kind of comedy based around pratfalls and mild comic violence — smacks in the head, pokes in the eyes, people falling down, etc.


Examples:
The Three Stooges
The Mask
The Naked Gun

Screwball

Definition

Screwball comedy is a subgenre of the romantic comedy genre. It was applied to films where everything was a juxtaposition: educated and uneducated, rich and poor, intelligent and stupid, etc.


Examples:
She's Funny That Way
Anchorman
Flirting with Disaster

Parody

Definition

A parody film or spoof film is a subgenre of comedy film that parodies other film genres or films as pastiches, works created by imitation of the style of many different films reassembled together.


Examples:
Airplane!
Spaceballs
Scary Movie

Black Comedy

Definition

Black comedy, also known as black humor, dark humor, dark comedy, morbid humor, or gallows humor, is a style of comedy that makes light of subject matter that is generally considered taboo.


Examples:
Heathers
Dr. Strangelove
Fargo

Romance

Historical Romance

Definition

Historical romance is a broad category of fiction in which the plot takes place in a setting located in the past with a lot of emphasis on what life is like in that time setting.


Examples:
Pride and Prejudice
Titanic
Sense and Sensibility

Romantic Drama

Definition

Two basic elements comprise every romance novel: a central love story and an emotionally satisfying and optimistic ending.



Examples:
A Star Is Born
Casablanca
La La Land

Romantic Comedy

Definition

Romantic comedy is a subgenre of comedy and slice-of-life fiction, focusing on lighthearted, humorous plot lines centered on romantic ideas, such as how true love is able to surmount most obstacles.


Examples:
Crazy, Stupid, Love
The Proposal
The Kissing Booth

Chick Flick

Definition

Chick flick is a slang term, sometimes used pejoratively, for the film genre catered specifically to women's interests, and is marketed towards women demographics.


Examples:
Legally Blonde
Clueless
She's The Man

Paranormal Romance

Definition

Paranormal romance is a subgenre of both romantic fiction and speculative fiction. Paranormal romance focuses on romantic love and includes elements beyond the range of scientific explanation.


Examples:
Crimson Peak
Shape of Water
About Time

Horror Genre

Zombie

Definition

This genre has featured zombies that are more agile, vicious, intelligent, and stronger than the traditional zombie. It brings about feeling about life, death, survival, seeking a cure zombies being the obstacle.

Examples:
Night of the Living Dead
World War Z
Train to Busan

Folk Horror

Definition

This genre generally – but not always – deals with rural, often British settings where the scares come not from an intrusive outsider.




Examples:
The VVitch
Midsommar
The Blair Witch Project

Body Horror

Definition

Horror that intentionally showcases graphic or psychologically disturbing violations of the human body. These violations may manifest through aberrant sex, mutations, mutilation, etc.


Examples:
Hellraiser
Tusk
The Fly

Found Footage

Definition

Found footage is a film subgenre in which all or a substantial part of the work is presented as if it were discovered film or video recordings.



Examples:
Cloverfield
The Blair Witch Project
V/H/S

Disaster

Definition

A disaster film or disaster movie is a film genre that has an impending or ongoing disaster as its subject and primary plot device.



Examples:
Geostorm
2012
The Wandering Earth

Thriller

Conspiracy Thriller

Definition

The protagonists of conspiracy thrillers are often journalists or amateur investigators who find themselves pulling on a small thread which unravels a vast conspiracy that ultimately goes "all the way to the top."

Examples:
Angels and Demons
National Treasure
The Davinci Code

Crime thriller

Definition

Crime films are often based on real events or are adaptions of plays or novels, or a remake or adoption of a previous film. Some plots are original and entirely fictional. It can be a gangster film.


Examples:
Goodfellas
Fargo
Scarface

Legal Thriller

Definition

A subgenre of thriller and crime fiction in which the major characters are lawyers and their employees. The system of justice itself is always a major part of these works.


Examples:
The Whole Truth
The Firm
The Lincoln Lawyer

Spy Thriller

Definition

Deals with the subject of fictional espionage, either in a realistic way (such as the adaptations of John le Carré) or as a basis for fantasy (such as many James Bond films).



Examples:
Goldfinger
Mission Impossible
Casino Royale

Psychological Thriller

Definition

Narratives with domesticated settings in which action is suppressed and where thrills are provided instead via investigations of the psychologies of the principal characters


Examples:
Pyscho
Silence of the Lambs
Get Out

Supernatural Thriller

Definition

Supernatural thriller films include an otherworldly element (such as fantasy or the supernatural) mixed with tension, suspense, or plot twists.



Examples:
Poltergeist
The Shining
IT

Techno Thriller

Definition

A movie in which the excitement of the plot depends in large part upon the descriptions of computers, weapons, software, military vehicles, or other machines.


Examples:
Tron
Ex Machina
I, Robot

Western

Classic Western

Definition

Western is a genre of fiction set primarily in the latter half of the 19th and early 20th century in the Western United States, which is styled the "Old West".




Examples:
The Good, The Bad, and the Ugly
The Kid
A Fistful of Dollars

Revisionist/ Anti-Western

Definition

The Revisionist Western, Anti-Western or Post-Western is a subgenre of the Western film that traces its roots to the mid-1960s and early-1970s.






Examples:
Django Unchained
True Grit
The Hateful Eight

Contemporary/ Neo Western

Definition

Uses traditional genre conventions and values but transplant them to a contemporary setting.






Examples:
The Three Burials of Melquiades Estrada
The Outlaw Josey Wales
Unforgiven

Fantasy Space Western

Definition

Space Western Science Fiction incorporates elements of the American West in a space setting.






Examples:
Cowboys Vs. Aliens
Outland
Flash Gordon

Modern Western

Definition

Following the success of Django Unchained, modern westerns play on western themes and setting but leave out dated aspects such as singing and dancing cowboys.



Examples:
True Grit
No Country for Old Men
Magnificent Seven

Sci-Fi Genre

Time Travel

Definition

Time travel in modern fiction is sometimes achieved by space and time warps, stemming from the scientific theory of general relativity. Stories from antiquity often featured time travel into the future through a time slip.


Examples:
Back to the Future
Groundhog Day
Edge of Tomorrow

Cerebral Science

Definition

A Cerebral film is one where plot is secondary to the dialogue of the film, perfect example is My dinner with Andre. it refers to the word intellectually, which involves the psychological processes of thinking.


Examples:
The Matrix
Inception
Donnie Darko

Robot/Monster

Definition

A monster movie, creature feature, or giant monster film is a film that focuses on a group of characters struggling to survive attacks by one or more antagonistic monsters, often abnormally large ones.


Examples:
Godzilla
King Kong
Pacific Rim

Alien Invasion

Definition

A common feature in science fiction stories and film, in which extraterrestrials invade the Earth either to exterminate and supplant human life, enslave it under an intense state, harvest people for food, etc.

Examples:
Independence Day
Invasion of the Body Snatchers
The War of the Worlds
